I decided to interview my 17 year old brother Jackson. Initially, I was going to interview my 14 year old brother. On the day of the interview he lovingly
and compassionate told me to screw off then hung up the phone. So I decided to interview Jackson on what it is like to be a high schooler in today’s day and age.



Interviewer: What is your name and age?

Jackson: You know my name and age, I’m your brother!

Interviewer: Jaaaackson, will you please just answer the question?! 
Jackson: (incoherent mumbles) Yes, my name is Jackson West and I am 17 years old.

Interviewer: What is it like for you to be in high school?

Jackson: I feel like right now high school is in a transitional stage, more or less. It is determining what our
education in the future is going to look like. Our class rooms are constructed for different learning styles, and more
based around technology rather than shutting it out. We even have a video call system set up in some of our langue
classrooms, and other schools that don’t have the money to hold that class now can. We are kinda like ginny pigs testing
out the waters of this new era of education. I guess you could call use trailblazers.

Interviewer: *small chuckles* Thats actually a really interesting point, and even contrasts to my own high school experience.
Moving on to the next question, who is your favorite teacher at school and why, how has this person changed your life, etc.?

Jackson: Mrs. Gregg, she is my teacher and debate coach. She has helped me breaking out of my shell of being nervous.
As well, overall be a better speaker and communicate a lot more. To get out there and get involved in high school. She
encourages everyone to mingle. The debate team is a debate family. She has affected my life in the way,
that if I never took debate I would not be the same.

Interviewer: That is amazing, I hope she knows that! Did you tell her that?! Jack that is so sweet and cute, you should tell
her that….awe… Okay, so what are some goals you would like to complete this year or in the near by future?

Jackson: Ahhh, long term… which is six months or more. I’ve been taught that, soooo. Basically I would like to get to nationals
and state champ for debate, I would like to speak sign langue to the extent I could communicate with someone,
as well as go to more games.

Interviewer: As you come to the end of your high school experience do you feel like you’ve had a “classic high school experience”,
with the homecoming, the football games, the prom king and queen, etc. Some high school musical shit, ya know?

Jackson: So I feel like I have had a modify version of that, where I am able to experience a wider range of things. I have so many friends
in every grade, and always have. Rather than just being friends with people your own age and feeling scared to interact with anyone
older than you. I am in multiple clubs, I go to school dances, and football games. I feel like I’ve had a well rounded experience of both
the traditional and non traditional.

Interviewer: Fantastic, I really appreciate your thoughtful answers. Before we end this interview I would like to ask you one more question.
If you could expel one stereotype about being a teenager today, what would that be?

Jackson: Uhhhhhhh, that all teenagers are entitled. I know a lot of people who work incredibly hard. They work two jobs, they are intelligent.
But because they are teenagers people don’t give them credit. People think they are entitled to think they are smart or hard working, whatever.
That “Oh they think they are successful now, but just you wait until they get into the real world.” People think that teenagers are smart, special,
etc just because their parents told them that, but I have found that not to be the case at all. And it all because of their age.
Also, we don’t all smoke dope.

Interviewer: Alrighty, then. Thank you Jackson for giving us some insight into your life. Love ya punk.

Jackson: You’re welcome, and you owe me lunch or you can just venom me.
